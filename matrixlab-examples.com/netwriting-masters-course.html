Effective Netwriting (writing for the net) is indeed something that anyone can accomplish. There is nothing magical or complicated about it. And you don't need an English degree to do it. But you do need to make the critical offline-to-online business mindshift from "location, location, location" to...

"Information, information, information"

Surfers on the Net are not looking for you or your business. People search for information and solutions. So give them what they want. Well before you make your first sale, provide the information (high value content) that people are searching for, in a way that the Search Engines like. Create content that overdelivers what your visitors seek.

Small businesses tend to fail on the Net because they prepare to sell and collect money, before they have provided what their visitors are searching for (information). These small business owners build a Web site to sell and somehow figure that traffic will just show up and be willing to buy or hire immediately...

Let's summarize these simple steps...
  •    Create the information that your prospective customers want.
  •    Attract targeted traffic at the Search Engines.
  •    Build confidence (people buy from those they trust).
  •    Convert traffic and provide your merchandise - whatever that may      be... hard goods, e-goods, services... even knowledge!
The first steps involve a completely different kind of Netwriting. Write to deliver what people want... information. And write for two audiences, the Search Engine spiders and the humans. That's what effective PREselling is all about!

Join the "Top 5%" on the Net. PREsell with information-packed content, then sell with benefit-focused copy, smoothly converting your visitors into customers. This "1-2" power strategy is the core to building traffic and earning income on the Net.
